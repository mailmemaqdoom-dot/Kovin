📵 Phone not working
This is usually recoverable
A phone that won't respond is often caused by a software crash, a failed update, or a deeply drained battery that shows no signs of life.
In most cases a forced restart or short charge brings it back. A quick diagnosis confirms the cause in minutes.
If it's a hardware fault, the repair cost is explained clearly before anything is touched.

Battery draining fast
🔋 Battery draining fast
Battery health may have reduced over time
After 300–500 charge cycles, most batteries lose the ability to hold a full charge. This is normal wear, not damage.
Replacing it usually restores normal use — the phone feels new and lasts through a full day again.
If the device is older, upgrading may be a better use of the money. That comparison is shown before anything is decided.

💔 Screen damaged
Screen repairs are common and usually same-day
A cracked surface, dark blotches, or a dead display each have different costs — explained clearly before any work begins.
Most screen replacements are done the same day using genuine or quality-equivalent panels.

Feels slower than usual
🐢 Feels slower than usual
Performance can almost always be recovered
Slowness is usually caused by full storage, background apps, or a battery throttling the processor to protect itself.
A software cleanup or battery swap is often all that's needed — a fraction of the cost of a new phone.
If the device is genuinely reaching its limit, honest guidance on upgrading is given — not a push to spend more.

Phone not working
📵 Phone not working
This is usually recoverable
A phone that won't respond is often caused by a software crash, a failed update, or a drained battery that won't show any sign of life.
In most cases a forced restart or a short charging period brings it back. A technician can confirm this in minutes.
If it's a hardware fault, the repair cost will be explained clearly before anything is done.

Battery draining fast
🔋 Battery draining fast
Battery health may have reduced over time
After 300–500 charge cycles, most batteries lose the ability to hold a full charge — this is normal wear, not damage.
Replacing it usually restores normal use. The phone will feel like new and last through a full day again.
If the device is older, upgrading may be a better use of the money — that comparison will be shown before anything is decided.

Screen damaged
💔 Screen damaged
Screen repairs are very common and usually quick
A cracked surface, dark blotches, or a completely dead display each have different repair costs — the difference will be explained before any work begins.
Most screen replacements are completed the same day. Genuine or quality-equivalent panels are used.
If the cost of repair approaches the value of the device, a better option will be suggested instead.

Feels slower than usual
🐢 Feels slower than usual
Performance can almost always be recovered
Slowness is usually caused by full storage, background apps, an old operating system, or a battery that's throttling the processor to protect itself.
A software cleanup or battery replacement is often all that's needed — and it costs a fraction of a new phone.
If the device is genuinely reaching its limit, honest guidance on upgrading will be given — not a push to spend more.
